CONTRACT MODULE — DEFINITION & SCOPE
1. Purpose of the Contract Module
The Contract Module manages all long-term commercial agreements between the service organization and customers (individuals, fleets, corporates), ensuring:
Predictable revenue
Controlled service entitlement
SLA enforcement
Full service-to-contract traceability
Financial and operational compliance
The module acts as the commercial backbone for AMC, warranty, fleet, subscription, and corporate service agreements.
2. Contract Types Supported
The system must support multiple contract models:
AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract)
Extended Warranty Contract
Fleet Service Contract
Subscription-Based Service Plan
Corporate / Institutional Contract
OEM / Dealer Service Agreement
Custom Contract (configurable)
Each contract type must be template-driven and configurable.
3. Contract Master Data
Each contract must have the following core attributes:
Contract Identity
Contract ID (system-generated)
Contract Type
Contract Status
(Draft → Active → Suspended → Expired → Terminated)
Branch / Region
Linked Customer(s)
Linked Vehicle(s) (single or multiple)
Validity & Coverage
Start date / End date
Coverage period (months / km / hours)
Renewal rules
Grace period configuration
Financial Terms
Contract value
Billing model:
One-time
Monthly / Quarterly / Annual
Tax structure
Discount / negotiated pricing
Penalty clauses (optional)
4. Service Entitlement Definition
Each contract must clearly define what is covered and what is not.
Coverage Rules
Covered service types
Covered labor operations
Covered spare parts
Consumables inclusion/exclusion
Free vs chargeable services
Visit limits (count-based)
Usage limits (km / hours)
SLA Parameters
Response time
Resolution time
Priority handling
Escalation rules
5. Contract → Service Workflow Integration
This is non-negotiable.
At Appointment Stage
Auto-detect active contract
Show contract eligibility badge
Display remaining entitlements
At Job Card Creation
Auto-apply contract pricing
Lock covered items as “Free / Included”
Highlight non-covered items
During Service Execution
Tag every task and part as:
Contract-covered
Partially covered
Non-covered
At Billing
Zero-value invoice for fully covered jobs
Split billing for partial coverage
Contract consumption summary shown
6. Contract Consumption & Traceability
Every service under a contract must be traceable.
Consumption Tracking
Service count used vs remaining
Parts consumed under contract
Labor hours consumed
SLA compliance per service
Traceability Mapping
Each service event must link to:
Contract ID
Service Tracking ID
Job Card ID
Invoice ID
This enables:
Audit
Dispute resolution
Renewal justification
7. Renewal, Upsell & Expiry Management
The module must actively drive retention.
Renewal Management
Expiry alerts (configurable days)
Auto-renewal rules
Renewal pricing logic
Renewal approval workflow
Upsell & Cross-sell
Suggest higher-tier contracts
Add-on services
Contract upgrades mid-term
8. Suspension, Exception & Termination Handling
Real-world contracts need flexibility.
Suspension
Temporary hold with reason
SLA pause
Reactivation workflow
Exception Handling
One-time goodwill services
Override with approval
Logged and auditable
Termination
Early termination rules
Penalty / refund calculation
Final settlement record

Contracts must follow maker–checker discipline.
Approval Workflow
Draft → Manager → Finance → Owner (configurable)
Version-controlled contract documents
Digital acceptance (customer confirmation)
Audit & Compliance
Contract version history
Change logs
Approval trail
SLA breach logs

Management Dashboards

Renewal pipeline

Under-utilized contracts
Profitability by contract
